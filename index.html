Story of Bastrop: Then and Now

here’s a tendency today to understand our lives in individualistic terms. We like to think about ourselves as unique personalities, distinct from everyone else. And to a large extent our own personal identities are important. But the life of the individual intersects with countless others innumerable times every day, and we can only realistically begin to understand who we are when we grasp our relationship to those around us. In metaphorical terms, then, a person’s life can be understood as a single strand of thread. It’s only when that single piece of thread, unique though it may be, comes together and interacts with others around it that a tapestry is formed. The tapestry image is important because it underscores not only the importance of each unique individual, but also the collective identity that can only come when these threads weave together to create a larger story. Bastrop has a long and storied community history. Yet that history can only be understood in terms of the individuals who make up our town; one cannot hope to be understood apart from the other. Bastrop: Then and Now is our attempt to bring these stories – the collective and the individual – together. Bastrop: Then and Now is also as much about listening as it is about story-telling. This is a project about listening to individual voices and hearing where they come into contact with others to form a larger, more comprehensive story. This project consists of nine distinct voices, but if you listen closely, you’ll hear them come together in unexpected ways. These stories all simultaneously stand alone and operate as a holistic entity. These stories are us, both then and now. It’s through listening to these stories that we believe we can begin to understand more about where we came from, where we are, and where we are going – as a community of individuals. .
